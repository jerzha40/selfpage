About
Mathematics → Automated Systems

I enjoy building long-lived infrastructure where real financial flows, data storage, and automation must stay consistent under constant change.

My background combines:

  • Mathematics (analysis, probability, time-series, mordern algebra)
  • Machine learning (ANN, CNN, RNN, G-CNN, PINN)
  • Low-level algorithms (FFT/NTT) and systems experiments

I am also the author of "Differential Informed Auto-Encoder" (arXiv:2410.18593), where I explore how to learn latent differential structures from data and reconstruct them via physics-informed neural networks. This experience shaped how I think about rigor and reproducibility in later engineering work.

Currently I am moving my focus from pure research into infrastructure for automated trading and personal finance, mainly through my ongoing projects Automata Virtual Machine (AVM) and regdel.

Key Projects
Selected personal work
Automata Virtual Machine (AVM)
2024–
A unified architecture for managing multiple trading “automata” across exchanges such as Hyperliquid, Binance, and dYdX. Currently the ABM automaton has been run live; other automata and the shared infrastructure are under active development. The design emphasizes decimal-precision arithmetic, centralized logging, and a common data layer for both real trades and backtests.

regdel – Ledger & Accounting Engine
2024–
A self-designed accounting system for tracking both personal and trading finances. regdel is built around algebraic ledger slices (semigroup structures) and a double-entry-inspired model, producing audit-ready logs that can be replayed and checked over long time spans.
